This is my daily mindfulness blog

What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

I learnt that 'mindfulness conversation' is not something that comes easily to me. I will definitely continue to work on this skill, especially in relation to mindful listening and giving people my full moment-to-moment attention. I really want to be more aware of my attention and ensure that when I am listening to a person, that they have my full attention and all of the space and time they need to express themselves.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

I like the 'do less than you can' technique. This way the exercise does not feel like a burden or too much of a challenge.

Any other musings?

I found the explanation of the difference between open and focussed attention really interesting. I am glad that there is value and benefit in training both forms of attention and also training your mind to switch easily between the two.
