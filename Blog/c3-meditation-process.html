This is my meditation process blog

How did the 'process over product' concept affect the way you tackled the site redesign and rebuild?

I think I probably struggled to implement this approach as much as I would have liked to. This is likely to be because I was wanting my webpage to look good and so my process in CSS was often based around getting my page to look better rather than engaging in the process.

What did you think about meditation before reading chapter 2?

I thought it was a practice that helped a lot of people with their state of mind which helps them to be more mindful throughout their day.

What new things have you learnt about meditation?

I did not realise so many scientific studies had drawn links between various cognitive functions, physical abiltiies, and even one's immune system.

Did any of the suggested meditation techniques stand out to you?

I like that they take a broad approach to meditation and recognise that you can walk and meditate at the same time. I also liked the analogy that meditation is similar to riding a bike in relation to your meta-attention in that as your meta-attention stregthens, your ability to recover wandering attention quickly and often improves as well. This creates the effect of continuous attention, which is concentration.

Any other musings?

I would like to see the book break down the traditional body/mind divide and the concept that the two function and are trained independently of each other. I tend to believe that the two are inextricably inter-related and that a healthy, happy body goes a long way to ensuring a healthy, happy mind, and vice versa.
